About Me

My Journey into the Cosmos

My fascination with the night sky began during a childhood camping trip in the Rocky Mountains. Away from city lights, I saw the Milky Way for the first time and was instantly captivated. That experience sparked a lifelong passion for astronomy.

I received my first telescope as a birthday gift at age 14—a modest 4.5-inch reflector. While the views were humbling and inspiring, I wanted to share what I was seeing with others. That desire led me to astrophotography.

For the past eight years, I've been honing my skills in capturing the night sky. What I love most about this hobby is how it combines technical challenges with artistic expression. Each successful image feels like a personal achievement and a connection to the universe.

Biggest Challenges in Astrophotography

Astrophotography comes with unique obstacles that make each successful image particularly rewarding:

  • Light Pollution: Finding truly dark skies often requires traveling far from cities
  • Weather: Clouds can ruin weeks of planning in minutes
  • Technical Complexity: Equipment requires precise alignment and calibration
  • Patience: Some targets require 20+ hours of total exposure time
  • Cost: Quality equipment represents a significant investment
